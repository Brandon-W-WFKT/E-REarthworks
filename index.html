The short version

Stop typing dockets by hand.

Today, a subbie texts in a photo of a handwritten docket, someone manually moves it from the office phone, keys it into MYOB, renames and files the photo and emails the invoice. The manual entry is the grind. That is what this removes.

Now the photo comes in, the details are read, and a sales order is waiting in MYOB. Nicky or Maia check it against the photo, convert it and send. The last step always stays with a person.

The work that disappears is the typing. The judgement stays with your team.

What to know

The honest bits.

None of these are dealbreakers. They are the things worth saying out loud now so there are no surprises later.

You see a detailed design first

Before any building, I share a clear design of the whole flow. It is your chance to fine-tune it, and it confirms the build effort and price are right before we commit.

MYOB's approval is a queue

Connecting to MYOB needs their developer sign-off, currently about a week. Outside my control, so I lodge it the day you say yes and build around it.

Your data stays in your systems

The flow runs inside your Microsoft 365. The only thing that leaves is the docket photo, read and returned, never used to train. Your bank feeds are never touched.

Clear photos read best

A clean photo reads almost perfectly, and anything illegible is flagged for hand entry, never guessed. We tune it on your real dockets as we build to cut the variations, and the human check before sending stays the control.

The docket is the truth

What is written on the docket is what gets charged. It transcribes faithfully, it does not check your pricing. Per-job pricing is expected and fine.

Nothing ever vanishes

Anything that cannot be processed automatically lands in the inbox, flagged. A little manual entry now and then is normal, not a fault.

What the price assumes

It assumes your MYOB customer and item records are broadly clean. I check a sample early. If they need real tidying, or volumes jump well past today, we scope and agree that before building.

The fixed price covers what's here

New ideas mid-build are welcome. They get scoped and priced separately so this number stays honest.
